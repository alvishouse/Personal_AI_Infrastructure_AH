## Source Content Summary

**Hook:** "Lean gave you PDCA. AI needs DARI."

**Key stat:** McKinsey 2025 — high performers are 2.8× more likely to redesign workflows before deploying AI. 55% vs 20%.

**The 4 DARI Stages:**

1. **Discover** — Map the work before you automate it. Identify every task that crosses a human-agent boundary. Document inputs, expected outputs, and what "done right" looks like.

2. **Architect** — Design the handoffs, not just the tasks.
   - Human-to-Agent: What does the agent need to do the job?
   - Agent-to-Human: What must a human verify before trusting the output?
   - Agent-to-Agent: What checkpoints exist between models in a chain?
   - Agent-to-System: What guardrails exist before data touches a live system?
   - Each handoff needs 3 properties: **clear, verifiable, recoverable**

3. **Run** — Execute with visibility. Log outputs. Flag deviations. Every agent needs a designated owner (same as a machine operator on a production line). Accountability doesn't disappear when the worker is a model.

4. **Improve** — Measure what's drifting, not just what's broken. Three drift types:
   - **Capability Drift**: the model itself changes
   - **Performance Drift**: outputs decline without any model change
   - **Context Drift**: your business changed but the agent didn't
   - Cadence: daily, weekly, monthly checks

**Positioning:** DARI isn't a replacement for Lean — it's Lean extended to the delegation problem.
