21,000 hours per year. Wasted.
In a 100-person company.

You know AI saves time. Your dashboards prove it. But the data on where that time goes should keep every operations leader up at night.

Four research studies tell the same story — and it is not the one your AI vendor is sharing. Here is the cascade.

1/ AI frees 5.7 hours per employee per week.

McKinsey surveyed CEOs and senior executives. The time savings are real. In a 100-person company, that is roughly 30,000 hours freed per year.

- 5.7 hours per employee per week (McKinsey)
- 285 hours per week in a 50-person team
- 14,820 hours per year per team
- 30,000 hours per year in a 100-person company

The freed capacity exists. The question is where it goes.

2/ Only 1.7 of those 5.7 hours reach value work.

McKinsey found that 4 out of every 5.7 freed hours evaporate. They get absorbed by more meetings. More email. More volume of the same low-value tasks. The time was saved. Then it was spent on nothing.

HBR confirmed it: 83% of AI users who saved time wasted at least a quarter of it. Among managers, 36% wasted more than half. The people who should be redirecting the freed capacity are the worst at doing it.

3/ The companies that reinvest outperform by 3.6x.

BCG studied 1,800 companies and found a widening AI value gap. "Future-built" companies — those pursuing innovation-core transformation — achieve:

- 1.7x more revenue growth
- 3.6x greater three-year total shareholder return
- 40% more cost savings than efficiency-focused laggards

The innovation-focused companies beat the cost-focused companies on cost. The efficiency-only play loses on its own terms.

4/ 56% of CEOs report zero financial impact from AI.

More than half. Not negative returns. Zero returns. The tools work. The deployment worked. The measurement worked. The strategy did not.

Because the strategy stopped at "we saved time" and never answered "what did we build with it?"

The pattern across all four studies is identical. The problem is not AI. The problem is not the technology. The problem is what happens after the efficiency win.

You have the freed hours. The data proves it.

The question is whether you are investing them or consuming them.

What is your Capacity Dividend going toward?
