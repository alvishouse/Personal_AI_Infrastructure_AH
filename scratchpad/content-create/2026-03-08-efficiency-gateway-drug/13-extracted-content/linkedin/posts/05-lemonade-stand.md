Two girls. One lemonade stand each.
Only one built a business.

Same neighborhood. Same summer. Same tools. You have probably been told to work like Girl A. That advice is costing you more than you think.

This simple story explains why most AI strategies fail — and what the winning companies do differently.

1/ Girl A optimizes speed.

She upgrades her juicer. She trains faster. She cuts cost per cup. She makes lemonade in half the time.

Girl A is running the denominator play. Faster inputs. Lower costs. More throughput. Her stand is more efficient than ever.

- Faster juicing
- Lower cost per cup
- Higher volume
- Same customers, same corner, same price

She works harder every day and her margins barely move.

2/ Girl B optimizes position.

She moves her stand in front of the hotel where thirsty tourists pay a premium. Same lemonade. Same effort. Completely different outcome.

Girl B is running the numerator play. She is not making lemonade faster. She is finding where thirsty people pay more. Her output value is higher because she changed what she was optimizing for.

3/ This is your AI strategy.

Management consultants learn one equation early in their training:

Productivity = Output Value / Input

Girl A shrinks the Input. Girl B raises the Output Value. Both work hard. Only one builds a business.

Every company running AI efficiency plays is Girl A. You are making lemonade faster. Your competitor — the one reinvesting freed capacity into customer-facing innovation — is Girl B. She moved the stand.

4/ The gap widens every quarter.

BCG studied 1,800 companies. The ones focused on innovation achieve 40% more cost savings than the ones focused on cost. They also deliver 1.7x more revenue growth and 3.6x greater shareholder return.

Girl A runs the treadmill. Girl B builds the springboard. Same tools. Same effort. Opposite trajectories.

The question was never about the lemonade.

It was about where you set up the stand.

Which girl are you?
