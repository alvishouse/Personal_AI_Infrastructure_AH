Most AI strategies fail in week 5.
Not because the tools stop working.

The tools work fine. The efficiency wins are real. But nobody planned for what to do with the freed time. So it vanishes into more meetings, more reports, more email.

Here is a 4-week plan that changes the trajectory. One week at a time. No extra budget required.

1/ Week 1: run the capacity dividend audit.

Calculate how many hours AI is freeing per week across your team. Every tool. Every automated workflow. Every process that runs faster.

- List every AI tool currently deployed
- Quantify freed hours per person per week
- Multiply by team size and 52 weeks
- Assign a fully loaded dollar value

McKinsey found AI frees 5.7 hours per employee per week. In a 50-person team, that is 14,820 hours per year. Present that number to leadership as available investment capital — not cost savings.

2/ Week 2: write the reinvestment thesis.

Before your next efficiency win lands, decide what you will build with the freed capacity. One page. Three sentences that matter:

"We will redirect X hours toward Y initiative, which will create Z value for customers."

Get leadership sign-off on the thesis, not just the efficiency project. Booking Holdings modeled $170M in reinvestment before their $150M in savings arrived. The decision came first. The results followed.

3/ Week 3: pilot the numerator play.

Direct one team's freed capacity toward one customer-facing project. Something that would not exist without the freed hours. Keep the scope tight: one team, one project, four weeks.

- Choose something customer-facing
- Protect the time — do not let it absorb back into volume work
- Track new value created, not just time saved
- Document what happens

This is your proof of concept. The experiment that shows your organization can redirect efficiency gains toward value creation.

4/ Week 4: build the new dashboard.

Add numerator metrics alongside denominator metrics. Track revenue from initiatives funded by freed capacity. Track customer satisfaction from new capabilities. Track offerings launched with reinvested time.

The QBR slide changes from "AI saved $400K" to "AI-freed capacity generated $1.2M in new revenue."

That is the dashboard that ends the treadmill permanently.

The companies with 3.6x greater shareholder return made this shift. They stopped counting what they saved. They started counting what they built.

Start with Week 1. This week. Sixty minutes.

What are you building with the time AI freed?
