## Tier Lists

### Tier 1 — Major AI/Operations Thought Leaders (10x follower leverage)

These are the five creator types whose content you should engage with 48 hours before posting
and within 30 minutes of posting your own content. Their audiences are your audience.

1. **AI transformation consultant with 100K–300K followers** — publishes weekly takes on GenAI ROI
   disappointment, enterprise AI adoption gaps, or "why your AI pilot failed." Typically a former
   McKinsey or BCG partner now running an independent practice. Posts frameworks, not vendor
   pitches. Leave substantive comments on their posts about GenAI ROI 2 days before your post goes
   live. Target: 3–4 comments per week from this tier.

2. **Chief Operations Officer or VP Operations at a $1B+ company with 50K–150K followers** — shares
   operational transformation war stories. Posts about systems thinking, org design, and efficiency
   paradoxes. The audience follows them for practitioner credibility. Engage on posts where they
   discuss AI-driven headcount or process changes — add the capacity reinvestment angle.

3. **Future-of-work researcher or workforce economist with 75K–200K followers** — cites McKinsey,
   HBR, and BCG regularly. Publishes data-heavy posts about productivity and AI's actual workforce
   impact vs. hype. Engaging here signals research fluency to their audience. Leave comments that
   extend their data points with the 5.7-hour / 1.7-hour McKinsey gap.

4. **Operations-focused venture capitalist or private equity partner with 80K–250K followers** —
   evaluates AI investments, publishes about where AI value actually accrues vs. where it is
   arbitraged away. Their audience includes CFOs and C-suite operators who are exactly your ICP.
   Engage when they post about AI portfolio company performance or ROI measurement.

5. **Business school professor or researcher specializing in digital transformation with 50K–120K
   followers** — publishes peer-reviewed insights in accessible format. Citing their work or
   engaging their posts on AI productivity paradoxes adds academic credibility to your contrarian
   angle. Engage specifically on posts about the productivity paradox or AI adoption curves.

These are creators with audiences similar in size to yours or 2–5x larger who publish content

2. **AI strategy consultant serving mid-market companies (15K–50K followers)** — publishes tactical
   AI implementation content. Not a big-name thought leader, but highly engaged niche following.
   Leave comments that add the Capacity Dividend lens to their efficiency-focused posts.

3. **COO or operations director at a SaaS or tech-enabled services company (8K–30K followers)** —
   discusses operational scaling, automation ROI, and team structure. Posts that reference
   McKinsey or BCG data are ideal engagement targets. Reply with the innovation-vs-efficiency
   paradox data point.

4. **Corporate training or L&D leader focused on AI upskilling (10K–35K followers)** — publishes
   about reskilling freed workers after automation. This is a direct adjacency to the Capacity
   Dividend concept. Engage on posts about what to do with human capacity post-automation.

5. **Digital transformation director or CTO at a professional services firm (5K–25K followers)** —
   posts about AI implementation, change management, and ROI measurement. Engage on posts where
   they share AI dashboards or KPIs — introduce the numerator vs. denominator framing.

6. **Operations-focused LinkedIn newsletter writer with 5K–20K subscribers** — publishes weekly
   on ops topics, often references the same research sources (McKinsey, BCG, HBR). Engage in
   the first hour of their posts to appear in their comment section while it is most visible.

7. **Mid-market CFO discussing AI investment strategy (8K–30K followers)** — posts about capital
   allocation, AI ROI, and technology investment decisions. The Booking Holdings reinvestment
   model is a direct hook into their worldview. Engage specifically on posts about AI as a cost

5. **HR director or Chief People Officer discussing AI's impact on workforce (2K–8K followers)** —

## Campaign Amplification Strategy

### 1. The Capacity Dividend Thread

After Post 03 (Framework) publishes on Mar 17, compile a LinkedIn article or newsletter-style
post that threads all the campaign concepts together: the BCG paradox, the QBR silence story,
the 4-step framework, and the Booking Holdings case study. Publish this as a LinkedIn article
(not a post) and link to it in the comment section of the Post 07 stats post on Mar 31. Articles
index differently in LinkedIn search and give the campaign a searchable home that lives beyond
the four-week posting window.

### 2. The Self-Assessment Hook

In the comment section of Post 04 (Myth-Buster), add a comment on Day 2 that poses a
self-assessment question: "Quick diagnostic: what does your current AI dashboard track?
(a) hours saved, (b) cost reduction, (c) what was built with freed capacity, or (d) all
three?" The multiple-choice format drives comment engagement and gives you live data on
where your ICP actually is in the capacity reinvestment journey. Reference the results
in Post 07 or Post 08.

### 3. Cross-Pollination with Prior Campaign

The Playing It Safe Illusion campaign (2026-01-31) and this campaign share an ICP and a
strategic theme: the hidden cost of playing it safe or optimizing the wrong variable. In Week
3 of this campaign, leave 2–3 comments on your own Playing It Safe posts linking the two
frameworks. Readers who engaged with that campaign but are not yet following this one will
get surfaced to the new content. Use the comment: "This connects to the Capacity Dividend
argument I published recently — the safety-optimization trap and the efficiency-optimization
trap have the same root."

### 4. Practitioner DM Sequence

After each post, identify the 5–10 highest-quality commenters — VP/Director-level operations
leaders who left substantive comments rather than just reactions. Send a direct message within
24 hours: "Thanks for engaging with the [post topic] — your point about [specific thing they
said] was the most useful addition to the thread. I'm building a resource on the Capacity
Dividend framework and would value your perspective on [one specific question relevant to their
comment]." This converts post engagement into conversations, and conversations into relationships
with exactly the ICP the campaign is designed to reach.
