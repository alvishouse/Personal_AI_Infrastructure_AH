Your team isn't slow. They're doing the wrong work.
The Repetition Tax is why.

Every operations team does. It's the hours spent on tasks that follow the same pattern every single time. Same steps. Same output. Same drain on your team's energy.

Here's what most leaders believe: about 20% of their team's hours go to this kind of work.

Here's what they find when they actually track it: 40-60%.

That gap is where your capacity problem lives.

---

The Repetition Tax Audit takes five days. Here's how to run it.

Give each person on your team a simple log. Two columns: task name, and one word—Judgment or Pattern.

Judgment: requires their experience, context, or decision-making. Only a person can do it.

Pattern: follows the same steps every time. Same input, same output, no thinking required.

That's it. Five days of honest tracking.

At the end of the week, add up the Pattern hours.

If you're like most operations teams, 30-50% of your team's paid time is going to tasks a well-designed intelligence layer could handle. Data entry. Report formatting. Status updates. Invoice matching. The list is long and familiar.

---

Why does this matter before anything else?

Because without this audit, you automate the wrong things.

Most teams skip straight to "what AI tool should we buy?" They implement something. It solves for 15% of the problem. The other 85% keeps grinding. The team is disappointed. The budget is spent. The CFO loses confidence.

The Repetition Tax Audit tells you exactly where the hours bleed. That's where you start.

The other thing it does: it builds the business case. When you show a CFO that 40% of your team's hours go to pattern-based work, the conversation about redirecting budget gets a lot easier.

What task does your team do most often that requires zero judgment to complete?
