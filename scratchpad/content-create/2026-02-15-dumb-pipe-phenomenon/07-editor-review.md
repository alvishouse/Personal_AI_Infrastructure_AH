## ⚠️ Areas for Improvement

### Issue 1: Hedge Words Found (3 instances)

**Location:** Multiple sections
**Problem:** Found 3 hedge phrases that weaken the Alvis voice:
- Line 67: "I watched that VP do what most operations leaders do" - implies uncertainty
- Line 242: "Because it worked for fifty years" - not a hedge, this is actually fine
- Line 297: "Most leaders find 30 to 50%" - "find" is softer than needed

**Fix:** Replace uncertain language with declarative statements
**Example:**
- BEFORE: "Most leaders find 30 to 50% of total hours are pattern-based"
- AFTER: "Thirty to fifty percent of total hours are pattern-based"

**Action Required:** Minor - only 1-2 actual hedge instances, voice is strong overall

---

### Issue 2: Over-Explanation in Framework Section

**Location:** Lines 104-150 (Framework section)
**Problem:** Step 3 ("Layer Intelligence") includes an extended electricity/appliance metaphor that, while good, slows momentum. The metaphor runs 4 sentences when the Alvis pattern would be 1-2 max.
**Fix:** Condense metaphor to single vivid statement, trust reader to extrapolate
**Example:**
- BEFORE: "Think about it like electricity. When electricity became cheap and reliable, the companies that won were not the ones building power plants. They were the ones building appliances -- products that created new value using commodity power. Your ERP, your CRM, your spreadsheets -- that is your infrastructure. The intelligence layer is the appliance that makes it useful."
- AFTER: "Think about it like electricity. The winners built appliances, not power plants. Your ERP and CRM are infrastructure. The intelligence layer makes them useful."

---

### Issue 3: Missing Immediate Context for Acme Manufacturing

**Location:** Line 138-139
**Problem:** "Acme Manufacturing" appears without industry context in first mention, then includes "midsize aerospace and automotive component manufacturer" - feels like an editing oversight
**Fix:** Standardize the introduction to match other examples
**Example:**
- BEFORE: "Acme Manufacturing, a midsize aerospace and automotive component manufacturer, layered AI-powered..."
- AFTER: "A midsize aerospace component manufacturer layered AI-powered..." (if keeping generic) OR give Acme a full proper introduction like other case studies

---

### Issue 4: Repetitive Phrasing in Close

**Location:** Lines 351-354
**Problem:** "The people who win" phrase appears twice in close proximity (line 345 and line 350). Alvis varies language even when repeating concepts.
**Fix:** Vary the second instance
**Example:**
- BEFORE: "The people who win at this are not the ones with the biggest budgets..."
- AFTER: "Winners aren't the ones with the biggest budgets..."

---

### Issue 5: Deck Copy Could Be Tighter

**Location:** Line 5-6 (Deck Copy)
**Problem:** "The strategy is not a secret. It is just uncomfortable." - The second sentence weakens the punch. Uncomfortable isn't the key insight; the willingness to redirect is.
**Fix:** Replace with action-oriented close
**Example:**
- BEFORE: "The strategy is not a secret. It is just uncomfortable."
- AFTER: "The strategy is not a secret. It requires redirecting headcount budget to intelligence."
