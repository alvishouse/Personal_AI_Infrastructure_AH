# Headline Sets for Cornerstone Content

**Date:** 2026-02-15
**Big Idea:** The Headcount Trap -- Why "Do More With Less" Is Actually the Right Strategy (Just Not the Way Your CFO Means It)
**Discovery Method:** Controversial Opinion
**Target Persona:** VP/Director of Operations & C-Level Executives at mid-market companies ($10M-$100M)

---

## Headline Set 1: The Controversial Reframe

**Eyebrow:** For Operations Leaders Told to "Do More With Less"

**Headline:** Your CFO Is Accidentally Right. You Do Not Need More People.

**Deck Copy:** Early AI adopters handle 55% more work with the same team. Laggards get 25%. The headcount you do not hire becomes the margin you keep.

**Elements Hit:**
- Curiosity: "Accidentally right" -- how?
- Pain Point: Budget rejected, team underwater
- Solution: Intelligence per person, not more persons
- Specificity: 55% vs 25% capacity gap
- Simplicity: One sentence hook
- Credibility: McKinsey data
- Big 4: New (new framing), Big (55% more output)

## Headline Set 4: The Permission Slip

**Eyebrow:** For VP Operators Working 60-Hour Weeks Who Cannot Get Budget Approval

**Headline:** Stop Asking for Headcount. Start Asking for Intelligence Budget.

**Deck Copy:** Redirect the salary of one unfilled position into AI-powered workflow automation. Show results in 30 days. Use the proof to fund everything else. A mid-market e-commerce company saved $600K this way.

**Elements Hit:**
- Curiosity: What is "intelligence budget"?
- Pain Point: Budget rejected, overworked, burning out
- Solution: Redirect unfilled salary to automation
- Specificity: One position's salary, 30 days, $600K savings
- Simplicity: Three-step formula in the subhead
- Credibility: Real company case study
- Time Frame: 30 days to results
- Big 4: Easy (one position redirect), Safe (low-risk pilot), New (new budget category)

## Headline Set 5: The Margin Killer

**Eyebrow:** The Math Your Board Has Not Seen Yet

**Headline:** Linear Scaling Is the Silent Killer of Mid-Market Margins

**Deck Copy:** Revenue doubled. Headcount doubled. Margin stayed flat. That is not growth. That is a treadmill. Companies adding intelligence instead of bodies return $3.70 for every dollar invested -- top performers hit $10.30.

**Elements Hit:**
- Curiosity: "Silent killer" and "math your board has not seen"
- Pain Point: Margin compression despite revenue growth
- Solution: Non-linear scaling through intelligence
- Specificity: $3.70 average return, $10.30 top performers
- Simplicity: Treadmill metaphor lands instantly
- Credibility: McKinsey ROI data
- Big 4: Big ($10.30 per dollar), New (non-linear scaling model), Safe (proven by top performers)
