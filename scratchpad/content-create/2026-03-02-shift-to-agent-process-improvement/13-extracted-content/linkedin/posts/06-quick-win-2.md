Gartner says 91% of AI models degrade over time.

Nobody told you how to catch it before the damage shows up in your numbers.

You deployed the agent. It worked. You moved on. That's the natural sequence when AI is treated as a project with a finish line. But agents don't stay calibrated the way a setting stays fixed. They drift. And drift is invisible until it isn't — until the customer complains, the model misfires on something expensive, or you pull the data and realize the last 90 days of output were quietly off-target.

The ICU analogy is exactly right here. You don't check a patient's vitals once and walk away. You monitor continuously, because the condition changes. Agents need the same treatment.

There are 3 types of drift. Each needs a different signal.

1/ Capability Drift: the model itself changed.
Your AI vendor updated the underlying model. Behavior shifted. This drift is external and invisible unless you're running benchmark tests against a known set of inputs. Catch it with: a monthly comparison of outputs against a fixed test suite.

2/ Performance Drift: outputs declined without any model change.
The model is identical. The results are worse. This usually traces to a process change — new input formats, new data sources, shifted volumes — that the agent was never informed of. Catch it with: weekly output quality spot-checks against your defined standard.

3/ Context Drift: your business changed but the agent didn't.
New products, new customer segments, new regulatory requirements. The agent is still answering last quarter's questions for this quarter's reality. Catch it with: a monthly business context review — does the agent's operating assumption still match the current environment?

Here's the monitoring cadence that costs under 3 hours per month.

- Daily: 2-minute output log scan — flag anything outside threshold
- Weekly: 15–30 minute quality review — spot-check samples against rubric
- Monthly: 1–2 hour assessment — compare model behavior, process changes, business context

Most teams skip all three. Then they spend weeks diagnosing a failure that a 2-minute daily scan would have caught in week two.

Start the cadence. Your agents will tell you what's wrong if you listen.
