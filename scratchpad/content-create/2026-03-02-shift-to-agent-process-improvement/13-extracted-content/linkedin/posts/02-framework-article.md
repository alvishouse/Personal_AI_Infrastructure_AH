Lean gave you PDCA. AI needs DARI.

And without it, your agents are running on a factory floor with no supervisor.

You wouldn't accept that on an actual production line. An unmonitored process with no standard, no feedback loop, and no owner would be a quality disaster waiting to happen. But that's exactly how most teams deploy AI agents — with good intentions and no architecture.

High performers aren't just using better AI tools. McKinsey 2025 found they are 2.8x more likely to redesign workflows before deploying. 55% vs. 20%. The discipline isn't in the model. It's in the method.

DARI is that method. Here's how each stage works.

1/ Discover: Map the work before you automate it.
Identify every task that crosses a human-agent boundary. Document the inputs, the expected outputs, and what "done right" looks like. If you can't define success, you can't detect failure.

2/ Architect: Design the handoffs, not just the tasks.
- Human-to-Agent: What does the agent need to do the job?
- Agent-to-Human: What must a human verify before trusting the output?
- Agent-to-Agent: What checkpoints exist between models in a chain?
- Agent-to-System: What guardrails exist before data touches a live system?

Each handoff needs three properties: clear, verifiable, recoverable.

3/ Run: Execute with visibility.
Deploy your agents inside a structure. Log outputs. Flag deviations. Every agent process needs a designated owner — the same way every machine on a production line has an operator. Accountability doesn't disappear when the worker is a model.

4/ Improve: Measure what's drifting, not just what's broken.
Agents degrade in three ways. Capability Drift: the model itself changes. Performance Drift: outputs decline without any model change. Context Drift: your business changed but the agent didn't. DARI gives you a cadence to catch all three — daily, weekly, monthly.

DARI isn't a replacement for Lean. It's Lean applied to the delegation problem.

You've spent years building process discipline. You already know how to design a system, measure its outputs, and close the loop. AI doesn't require you to abandon that thinking.

It requires you to extend it.
