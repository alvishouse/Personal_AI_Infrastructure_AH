Klarna cut 5,500 jobs to prove AI could replace them.

Then they quietly started rehiring. Here's what they left out.

This is the part of the Klarna story that gets left out of the LinkedIn victory laps. Yes, they cut headcount from 8,500 to 3,000 using AI. Yes, the efficiency metrics looked impressive on a slide deck. And then the quality of customer interactions collapsed.

By May 2025 they were rehiring. Not because AI failed — but because nobody had designed the system where AI and humans work together. They automated delegation. They didn't architect it.

Klarna isn't unique. A major bank offers an equally expensive lesson.

Their credit risk model ran at 95% accuracy. That's excellent. Nobody touched the model. But eight months later, accuracy had dropped to 87%. Leadership blamed the AI. Blamed the vendor. Brought in consultants. The model was fine.

The process had changed — new loan products, new customer segments, new data inputs. Nobody had built the feedback loop to detect that the context had shifted. The agent kept scoring risk against reality that no longer existed.

That's Context Drift. And it's one of three ways agents silently fail.

Both stories share one root cause: treating AI deployment as a one-time technical event instead of an ongoing operational discipline. The technology worked. The architecture was missing.

Here's what the teams who got it right did differently.

- They mapped every handoff before deploying — who hands to whom, what gets verified, what happens when it breaks.
- They built monitoring that watches for drift, not just crashes — daily signals, weekly reviews, monthly assessments.
- They kept humans in the loop at the decision points that mattered, not as a safety net for everything.
- They treated the agent like a new team member on a production line — capable, but requiring standards, oversight, and a feedback loop.

The result is not fewer humans. It's better-deployed humans working alongside better-governed agents.

Klarna learned this expensively. You don't have to.
