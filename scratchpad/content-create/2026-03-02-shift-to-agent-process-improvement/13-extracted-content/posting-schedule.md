## STRATEGIC NOTES & SUCCESS FACTORS

### Why This Schedule Works

1. **Priority Post Sequencing:** Posts 1 & 4 (authority + myth-buster) drive awareness; Posts 3 & 7 (story + case study) provide proof; Posts 5, 6, 8 educate and activate.

2. **Cadence (3x/week):** Prevents audience fatigue while maintaining visibility. Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday/Friday sequencing ensures consistent presence without weekend posting (when professional audience engagement drops).

3. **Tier 1 Engagement Strategy:** By engaging Tier 1 creators 24-48 hours before your posts launch, you plant seeds. When your posts go live, they're more likely to notice and comment because you've already been in conversation with them.

4. **Comment SLA (1 hour):** Rapid response signals engagement and builds loyalty. LinkedIn algorithm favors posts with early, consistent engagement. Your rapid replies trigger notifications, driving secondary engagement.

5. **Week 2 vs. Week 3 Strategy:** Week 2 introduces implementation posts (Posts 5-6) to move readers from "awareness" to "consideration." Week 3 (no new posts) is maintenance mode, deepening conversations and pushing cornerstone clicks.

### Key Risks & Mitigation

**Risk 1: Week 2 posts get low engagement**
- Mitigation: Quick Win posts (5-6) are tactical, not viral. They drive quality comments over volume. Continue reciprocal Tier 2 creator engagement to maintain visibility.

**Risk 2: No Tier 1 creators engage with your posts**
- Mitigation: Unlikely if you've been engaging with them consistently. If they don't comment, continue engaging with their content (don't stop). Value the relationship over the transactional engagement.

**Risk 3: You miss the 1-hour comment response SLA**
- Mitigation: This is critical for algorithm performance. Set phone reminders. Batch-respond every 2 hours if you can't do 1-hour. Quality of response matters more than speed, but speed matters.

**Risk 4: Tier 3 creators don't engage**
- Mitigation: Tier 3 engagement is about building a community, not immediate reach. Treat it as long-term relationship building. Value the quality of discussion over volume.
