## New Issues Found in V2

### Minor Issue 1: Identity filter placement — comes after "Here is how it works"

**Location:** Hook section, lines 30-47
**Problem:** The structure currently reads: (1) the three deliverables, (2) "Agentic Process Architecture" named, (3) "Here is how it works," and then (4) the identity filter. The identity filter arrives after the "Here is how it works" beat, which is typically the hook's closing line. This means the reader hits the forward bridge before being confirmed as the right audience. It is a sequencing question, not a content problem — the identity filter is strong, it just appears after the action cue rather than before it.
**Impact:** Low. The ICP reader will recognize themselves in the identity filter regardless of placement. The "here is how it works" beat is strong enough to hold without the identity filter preceding it. This is a polish note, not a blocking issue.
**Suggested fix:** Move the identity filter and challenges/transformations block to immediately follow the first paragraph ("You have no process discipline for the thing that will reshape all three: handing work to AI agents."), before introducing the three deliverables. This is a structural preference, not a requirement.
**Severity:** Non-blocking.

---

### Minor Issue 2: The "Without Flow Bridges" list is a slight over-explanation after the tollbooth metaphor

**Location:** Flow Bridges section — five-item list starting "Without Flow Bridges, five things happen predictably."
**Problem:** The tollbooth metaphor (calibrate to traffic, EZ-Pass vs. full stop) already teaches the concept effectively. The five-item consequence list then explains what happens when you remove the tollbooth — but the reader has already made this inference from the metaphor. This is a borderline case. The list reinforces, but does not teach. The voice guide says "refusal to over-explain" and "trust the reader to make connections."
**Impact:** Low. The list is not offensive — it is accurate and specific. Some ICP readers will find the explicit list useful for presenting upward. But it slightly weakens the metaphor by explaining what it already showed.
**Suggested fix:** Cut the five-item list or reduce it to the two most counterintuitive consequences (agents overreach — yes; humans over-review and become bottlenecks — this is the one the ICP does not expect and it lands hard). Leave the rest to the metaphor.
**Severity:** Non-blocking.

The CTA reference to "the Ladder" in the context of the AI Readiness and Adoption Program is sufficient for a first-touch article. A reader who wants the Ladder will ask. This is not a fix needed for publication.

## What the Draft Does Especially Well

**1. The compounding math is the standout moment.**
"If each agent in a three-step pipeline performs at 90% accuracy, the pipeline delivers 72.9% accuracy (0.9 x 0.9 x 0.9). Add a fourth agent and you drop to 65.6%." This is the moment the ICP reader stops nodding and starts doing the math on their own pipeline. It is reproducible, quotable, and directly translatable to executive presentations. A VP of Operations will write this on a whiteboard the same day they read it.

**2. The three-drift case study walkthrough is structurally elegant.**
The case study does not just show a result — it teaches the taxonomy by demonstrating it. Week 3 is Performance Drift, Week 6 is Capability Drift, Week 8 is Context Drift. A reader who skimmed the taxonomy explanation will have internalized all three categories by the time the case study ends. The chef analogy ("no single plate sends back, but regulars start noticing") for Performance Drift is particularly strong — it makes the gradual-fade concept feel familiar and human rather than technical.

**3. The close earns its confidence.**
"You now have the name for the thing that has been frustrating you." This is the correct payoff for the opening hook ("You have no process discipline for..."). The article opens by naming a gap and closes by confirming the reader now has the name for it. That is a complete arc. The final line — "The only question left is which workflow you start with" — assumes action without demanding it. Warm challenge. Not a hard sell.

**4. The tollbooth metaphor is the right level of concreteness.**
"Think of it as a tollbooth on a highway. You do not eliminate tollbooths — you calibrate them to the traffic. Some lanes get an EZ-Pass. Some require a full stop." This grounds an abstract concept in physical infrastructure the reader has literally driven through. The metaphor carries three points at once: there is a designed handoff, the depth of scrutiny varies by stakes, and the goal is calibration not elimination. One metaphor doing the work of three paragraphs.

**5. "So let's build it." as the evidence-to-framework bridge.**
This single sentence is a significant improvement over v1. It acknowledges the weight of the evidence just presented, signals forward motion, and drops the reader into the framework with momentum. It sounds like a person talking, not a document transitioning sections. This is the voice at its best.
