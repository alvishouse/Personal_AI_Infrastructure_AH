AI is not a model decision.
It is a refinery decision.

Most organizations spend the bulk of their AI budget on Layer 1 — the models. Claude. GPT-4o. Copilot. They subscribe, deploy, and wait for transformation.

Transformation does not come.

Here is what the data says about where they actually are: 95% of AI pilots never deliver measurable P&L impact. 89% of executives report no productivity gain despite AI adoption. 56% of CEOs say they have gotten nothing out of their AI investments.

Same models. Same data access. Same technology generation. Opposite results.

There is a concept from the oil industry that names this exactly.

📌 1 - THE CRACK SPREAD

In oil refining, the crack spread is the gap between the price of crude oil and the value of the refined products that come out of the refinery.

In June 2022, US refinery crack spreads peaked at $61.95 per barrel. The crude was worth one thing. The refined product was worth dramatically more. The refinery captured all the value.

Your organization has a crack spread too.

It is the gap between raw AI output and a refined business decision. Two companies subscribe to the same AI platform. One routes the output through designed workflows, governance protocols, and decision processes.

The output becomes a revenue-generating business decision. The other gets a chatbot response that sits in a browser tab.

Same crude. Different refinery. Different margin.

📌 2 - WHERE THE INTELLIGENCE LAYER IS NOW

Anthropic added $6 billion in annualized revenue in a single month. February 2026. The gap between Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini, and DeepSeek is narrowing. The intelligence layer is commoditizing.

Fighting over which AI model to use is fighting over drilling rights. The drillers who had the richest wells in 1872 Pennsylvania watched their market power evaporate. A refiner in Cleveland quietly bought every conversion facility in the region.

I have seen this same pattern inside organizations that have deployed AI for two or three years. The tools are running. The outputs are being generated.

Nothing is converting to decisions.

📌 3 - THE ORGANIZATIONAL CRACK SPREAD

The gap between your AI outputs and your business decisions is your crack spread.

• What percentage of AI output reaches a decision-maker in a useful form?
• How many of those outputs change a decision?
• Who tracks the outcome?

Most organizations find fewer than 20% of AI outputs reach a decision context. That number is your crack spread. That is the size of the refinery opportunity sitting in your existing AI investments.

The model is not the bottleneck.

The refinery is missing. Build it.
