Stop comparing AI models.
That is the wrong race.

Your LinkedIn feed is full of it. GPT-4o vs Claude vs Gemini. Benchmark comparisons.

Model release notes. Enterprise procurement committees evaluating vendors based on capability matrices.

Meanwhile the organizations pulling ahead are not doing any of that.

📌 1 - THE COPILOT GRAVEYARD

A mid-market operations team buys Copilot licenses for 200 employees.

Layer 1 investment. Significant budget. Leadership expects transformation.

🔻 90 days later: usage sits at 12%
🔻 The team blames the technology
🔻 The CFO questions the investment
🔻 The CIO defends the purchase
🔻 Everyone argues about the tool

The technology is fine. No one designed how AI output would flow into their existing approval workflows, communication chains, or reporting processes.

200 people received access to crude. Not one of them was given a refinery.

Deloitte's 2026 data confirms the pattern: worker access to AI rose 50% in 2025, but fewer than 60% of workers with access use it daily. Access without workflow integration produces no value.

📌 2 - WHO IS ACTUALLY WINNING

BCG's research is unambiguous: AI leaders generate 76% higher impact match between where AI is deployed and where it delivers results.

The leaders are not winning on model selection. They are winning on conversion infrastructure.

Roland Berger's Industrializers — the 10% of companies getting consistent AI returns — start with narrow, workflow-specific use cases. Domain leaders own implementation. AI is embedded directly into existing processes and data flows.

These are refinery decisions, not model decisions.

📌 3 - THE ROCKEFELLER PARALLEL

In 1872, the Pennsylvania drillers had the best technology, the richest deposits, the most active wells.

Rockefeller had the refinery.

By 1879, the drillers' market power had evaporated. Not because a better technology replaced theirs. Because the chokepoint — the conversion layer — was owned by someone else.

The model war is the driller's race. Optimizing drilling technology while a refiner builds the conversion layer is the exact mistake the Pennsylvania drillers made.

📌 4 - WHAT TO DO INSTEAD

• Audit Layer 2 before buying more Layer 1
• Name a workflow owner before naming a preferred model
• Measure conversion rate of AI outputs to business decisions, not adoption rate of AI licenses

The organizations winning right now are not winning on models. They built the conversion layer. They built the refinery.

The model war is the wrong race. Stop running it.
