Do this in 2 hours.
Your AI crack spread will be visible.

You have deployed AI tools. The C-suite is asking for ROI. You have dashboards full of outputs.

You cannot easily say what any of them produced.

That is not an AI problem. That is a refinery problem.

The Crack Spread Audit takes two hours. It will show you exactly where the conversion chain breaks in your organization. Which AI investments are sitting unrefined right now.

Here is how to run it.

📌 1 - PULL YOUR AI OUTPUT LIST

Before you audit, you need the inventory.

• List every AI tool your organization has active licenses for
• For each tool, write down: what outputs it generates and where those outputs go
• Include dashboards, email summaries, generated reports, flagged alerts, recommendations

This takes 30 minutes. Most leaders find 6–10 active AI outputs they cannot immediately explain the destination of.

📌 2 - THE THREE-QUESTION AUDIT

For each output, answer three questions:

• Does this output reach a decision-maker in a useful form?
• Does it change a decision?
• Does anyone track the outcome?

Run through the whole list. Do not stop to fix anything yet. Just score.

📌 3 - SCORE EACH OUTPUT

🔻 Crude — the AI produces output that sits in a dashboard, inbox, or browser tab. No one acts on it consistently. No tracking.
🔻 Partially Refined — the output reaches a human, but there is no decision protocol. Used sometimes. Not used sometimes. No governance.
💡 Refined — the output consistently feeds a documented decision process. The decision is made. The outcome is measured. The feedback loop is closed.

📌 4 - READ YOUR NUMBER

The percentage of outputs scored Crude is your organizational crack spread.

Most organizations find 80% or more of their AI output is crude. That number is not a failure. That number is the size of the opportunity sitting inside your existing AI investments.

You do not need a new model. You need a refinery for the outputs you are already generating.

Start with the one Crude output closest to a high-stakes decision.

That is your first refinery build.
