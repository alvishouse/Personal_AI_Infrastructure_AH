Name your AI refinery owner
in the next 24 hours.

You have AI tools running. Outputs are being generated. And if someone asked you right now — "who owns the conversion of those outputs into business decisions?" — you would hesitate.

That hesitation is the answer.

Only 1 in 3 organizations has a team dedicated to maintaining AI workflows. Two in three have no one accountable for whether AI outputs reach decisions at all.

That is the equivalent of running a refinery with no plant manager.

📌 1 - WHAT THE REFINERY OWNER ACTUALLY DOES

This is not an IT role. This is not a vendor relationship manager.

• Owns the conversion layer — the workflows, routing, governance, and decision protocols
• Works across functions — not siloed in one department
• Accountable for the organizational crack spread: what percentage of AI outputs produce measurable business outcomes
• Wakes up thinking about throughput, conversion efficiency, and output quality

HBR identifies seven structural frictions causing AI's last-mile failure. MIT Sloan confirms that none of them can be resolved by any single function acting alone. The refinery owner works across all of them.

📌 2 - WHAT HAPPENS WITHOUT ONE

🔻 No one measures whether AI outputs are reaching decisions
🔻 IT claims the territory. IT owns the pipeline, not the refinery
🔻 Every AI implementation is someone's side responsibility and no one's primary accountability
🔻 The refinery runs without a plant manager. Or does not run at all

📌 3 - HOW TO NAME ONE IN 24 HOURS

One question. Ask it in your next leadership conversation:

Who owns the conversion of our AI output into business decisions?

If no one answers, you have found your first refinery build. That silence is not a people problem. It is a structural gap. It is the most common reason AI investments produce no measurable return.

📌 4 - THE FIRST WEEK

• The new owner runs the Crack Spread Audit — score every AI output crude, partially refined, or refined
• They identify the highest-value decision that AI currently feeds inconsistently
• They document the conversion process for that one decision
• They own it from that week forward

The refinery with no plant manager is not a refinery.

It is expensive hardware and unrealized margin.

Name the owner today.
