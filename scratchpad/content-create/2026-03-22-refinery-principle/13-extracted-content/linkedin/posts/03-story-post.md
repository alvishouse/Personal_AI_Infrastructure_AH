Rockefeller never drilled a single well.
He controlled 90% of US oil refining.

January 1872. The American oil industry is in chaos. Crude prices have collapsed.

Producers in Pennsylvania are going bankrupt. Everyone with capital is racing to drill more wells — convinced that the answer is more crude, cheaper crude, faster crude.

One man in Cleveland sees the problem differently.

📌 1 - THE INSIGHT THE DRILLERS MISSED

John D. Rockefeller is not an oil driller. He is a refiner.

And he has noticed something the drillers have not: crude oil is worthless until it is refined. The drilling rig is not the bottleneck. The refinery is.

💡 The entire value of the oil industry flows through one chokepoint — the conversion of crude into kerosene, lubricants, and usable products
💡 Whoever controls that chokepoint controls the industry
💡 The drillers are fighting over the wrong layer

Rockefeller does not invest in more wells. He does the opposite.

📌 2 - WHAT HE DOES INSTEAD

In the first three months of 1872, Rockefeller buys out, shuts down, or bankrupts 22 of his 26 Cleveland competitors.

Ninety days. Twenty-two acquisitions.

He negotiates railroad shipping rates 40–50% below what smaller refiners can access, making his cost structure unbeatable. He presents competitors with his cost data, then offers a generous buyout. Most accept.

By 1879, Standard Oil refines over 90% of all oil in the United States.

The crude producers — the ones with the best drilling technology, the richest deposits, the most active wells — watch their market power evaporate. They had raw material. They had no path to market value.

📌 3 - THE AI PARALLEL

That pattern is repeating right now.

The AI model providers are the crude producers. They are racing to pump more intelligence — faster, cheaper, smarter.

Anthropic added $6 billion in annualized revenue in a single month. The intelligence is abundant.

The organizations building the refinery layer — the workflow infrastructure that converts AI output into business decisions — are making Rockefeller's play.

They are not competing on model selection. They are building the conversion layer. And whoever controls the conversion layer controls everything downstream.

The competitors had the best drilling technology. None of it mattered without refinery access.

Stop drilling. Build the refinery.
