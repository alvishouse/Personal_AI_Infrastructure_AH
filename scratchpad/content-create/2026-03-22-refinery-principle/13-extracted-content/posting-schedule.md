## Posting Order

The sequence is deliberate: lead with the highest-engagement formats (authority, story, myth-buster) to build momentum and prime the audience, then deliver the deep framework to a warm audience, then convert with case study + contrarian, and close with actionable quick wins.

**Rationale for sequence:**
- Posts 1–3 (Tue / Thu / Tue) build the conceptual foundation. Authority post names the problem. Story post gives it emotional weight. Myth-buster kills the dominant objection.
- Post 4 (Thu, Week 2) delivers the full framework to an audience already primed by the preceding three posts. The 4-Layer Model performs best when readers already feel the pain it diagnoses.
- Posts 5–6 (Tue / Thu, Week 2) provide proof and contrarian challenge. By this point in the sequence, the audience is warm — they have the concept, they have seen the historical story, they understand the fallacy. Case study provides evidence. Contrarian take challenges the alternative.
- Posts 7–8 (Tue / Thu, Week 3) close with action. Quick wins perform best with a primed audience because readers who have followed the campaign are ready to act, not just learn.

## 30/30/30/10 Engagement Framework

### Tier 1 — High-Follower AI/Operations Creators (30% effort)

Engage intensively with these creator types — 3 to 5 creators per week, meaningful comments (3+ sentences) on their content before and after posting.

📌 **Chief AI Officers / Chief Digital Officers** — post frequently about AI transformation challenges, model selection, and enterprise AI rollouts. Comment with the refinery reframe: "I see this as a Layer 2 gap — the model is rarely the bottleneck in the implementations I've studied."

📌 **Operations / Supply Chain thought leaders** — their audience overlaps directly with the manufacturing case study (Post 06). Engage on predictive maintenance, workflow design, and operational efficiency content.

📌 **AI Strategy Consultants at Big 4** (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, Accenture AI practices) — they cite the same research (BCG 76% leader-laggard gap, Deloitte 60% daily usage) and their followers are the exact target audience. Agree on the data, extend with the refinery framework.

📌 **Enterprise Software Executives** (Microsoft, Salesforce AI, ServiceNow) — they live in the Copilot Graveyard reality. Comment on their posts when they discuss adoption: "The adoption challenge you're describing is what I'd call a Layer 2 gap — the pipeline (Layer 1) is working, but there's no refinery to convert outputs to decisions."

📌 **CEOs of mid-market companies** who post about AI implementation — they are the exact leadership audience for the Quick Win posts. Engage before Post 7 goes live.

### Tier 2 — Peer-Level Creators (30% effort)

Engage consistently (2+ sentences) with 5 to 8 creators who have 2,000–15,000 followers and post regularly on AI, operations, or digital transformation.

📌 **AI Program Directors / Implementation Leads** — operational-level AI practitioners who are living the Layer 2 gap daily. Their followers are mid-funnel audiences for the Case Study and Framework posts.

📌 **VPs of Operations** in manufacturing, logistics, professional services — the 34% downtime reduction stat resonates immediately with this group. Engage on operational efficiency content.

📌 **Digital Transformation Consultants** (independent) — they advise the organizations in the target audience. Their comments on this content carry credibility signals to their own networks.

📌 **Business History / Strategy Writers** — for the Story Post (Post 02), engage with creators who write about business history, strategy, and leadership. The Rockefeller angle plays well in this community.

📌 **AI Ethics / AI Governance practitioners** — the Layer 2 / governance framing resonates with this community. Engage on workflow governance and AI accountability content.

📌 **CFOs / Chief Strategy Officers** — financial decision-makers who control AI budget. The crack spread concept (oil industry financial framing) is the most accessible entry point for this group.

📌 **Change Management specialists** — the Driller's Fallacy myth-buster (Post 03) is directly in their domain. Engage before and after that post goes live.

📌 **HR / People Operations leaders at AI-adopting organizations** — they are living the Copilot Graveyard. Engage on AI adoption and employee tool usage content.

### Tier 3 — Smaller High-Engagement Creators (30% effort)

Engage regularly (1–2 sentences) with 10 to 12 creators who have under 2,000 followers but consistently generate 5%+ engagement rates (Golden Ratio of 10%+). These are the comment-heavy micro-communities.

📌 **AI practitioners sharing implementation war stories** — small follower count, high comment density. Engage authentically with refinery observations: "The pattern you're describing is what I call the Driller's Fallacy — the gap isn't the tool, it's the conversion layer."

📌 **Operations managers at manufacturing companies** — direct overlap with Case Study post audience. Comment with genuine insight on their operational challenges.

📌 **Mid-career enterprise technology leaders** (10–15 years experience) — they have lived through enough technology transitions to feel the electricity parallel in the Myth-Buster post. Engage on lessons-learned content.

📌 **Strategy and MBA-adjacent content creators** — comment on business model and competitive strategy content with the Rockefeller chokepoint parallel.

📌 **AI tool reviewers / Product evaluators** — engage on model comparison content with the contrarian take: "This is the right question to ask about the tool — but the bigger question is whether the refinery exists to convert its outputs to decisions."

📌 **Startup operators** — they are building the tools at Layer 1; engage on product-market fit and workflow integration content.

📌 **Industry-specific AI content creators** (healthcare AI, fintech AI, manufacturing AI) — the case study post resonates most in these verticals. Engage with vertical-specific workflow observations.

📌 **Coaches / Advisors to mid-market executives** — they carry the refinery message to the exact C-suite audience this campaign targets. Engage on executive AI readiness content.

📌 **Academic / Research-oriented AI creators** — the NANDA/MIT/BCG/McKinsey research citations create common ground. Engage on AI research sharing content.

📌 **Newsletter writers covering enterprise technology** — they curate and amplify. A comment that frames the refinery principle clearly may earn a newsletter mention.

📌 **First-time AI deployers** sharing their early experience — they are in the Crude phase. Engage with empathy and the three-question audit: "The question I'd ask: does that output reach a decision-maker? If not, that's the first refinery build."

📌 **Operations-focused investors / VCs** — they fund the organizations that need the refinery. Engage on portfolio company AI implementation content.

### Own Content (10% effort)

Reply to every comment within 60 minutes of posting for the first 3 hours. After that, reply to every new comment within 24 hours.

**Reply strategy by post type:**

- **Authority Post (01):** When commenters share their own crack spread observations, respond with a diagnostic question: "What's the one decision in your org where AI outputs are closest to reaching the right person?"
- **Story Post (03):** When commenters engage with the Rockefeller angle, extend the analogy to their specific industry. Personalization drives reply depth.
- **Myth-Buster (04):** When commenters push back ("but the model does matter"), acknowledge the partial truth: "Model quality matters — but it's a Layer 1 constraint, not a Layer 2 constraint. Most orgs have enough crude. They need the refinery."
- **Framework Post (02):** When commenters self-identify their layer, ask them what the diagnostic question surfaces for them. Self-identification comments are the most shareable comment type.
- **Case Study (06):** When industry-specific commenters engage, ask about their own workflows: "What does your alert-to-action path look like right now?"
- **Contrarian Take (07):** When defenders of model selection engage, reframe: "I'd agree model quality matters for specific use cases — I'm questioning whether it should be the primary investment decision before Layer 2 is built."
- **Quick Win (05):** When commenters share their crack spread scores, amplify them: "80% crude is the industry average — what's the one output closest to a high-value decision you could refine first?"
- **Quick Win (08):** When commenters share who they named as refinery owner, celebrate publicly and ask what the owner's first 48-hour action was.
