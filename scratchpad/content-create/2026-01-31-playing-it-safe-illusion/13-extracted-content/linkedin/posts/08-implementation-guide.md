# Your 4-Week Path from "Watching AI" to "Learning from AI"

Understanding is not the same as doing.

Here's how to move from insight to action in four weeks—without a billion-dollar budget or a perfect strategy.

---

## WEEK 1: Audit the Truth

**Primary task:** Map where AI is already happening in your organization—with or without you.

According to Salesforce's research, 71% of employees are using AI tools regardless of corporate strategy.

This isn't a prediction. It's almost certainly already true in your organization.

Your job this week: Find it.

### The 3-Question Audit

Talk to people who interact with customers. Ask:

1. "What AI tools are you using?"
2. "What problems are those tools solving?"
3. "What would you lose if they disappeared?"

**Critical framing:** This isn't an enforcement exercise. It's a learning exercise.

Shadow AI tells you two things:
→ Where your organization has pain points that AI could address
→ Where your people are already learning (learning you could be capturing)

### By End of Week 1, You Should Have:

- [ ] Map of where AI is being used unofficially
- [ ] List of 3-5 problems those tools are solving
- [ ] Identification of 2-3 early adopters who are ahead of the curve

**This is your roadmap.**

---

## WEEK 2: Identify Your First Partners

**Primary task:** Find 1-3 promising AI partners. Not proven giants. Promising players.

The instinct will be to look for established vendors with case studies and testimonials.

Resist this instinct.

By the time an AI company has case studies, you've lost the learning advantage.

### What to Look For

→ Companies solving problems specific to your industry
→ Tools your shadow AI audit revealed employees already value
→ Startups with strong technical teams and emerging traction
→ Partners where your data or use case would be valuable to them

**The goal isn't to find partners who have proven themselves elsewhere.**

**The goal is to find partners who could prove themselves WITH you—and who would remember that you took a chance on them.**

### Budget Reality Check

Set initial budgets that assume failure.

If you'd be devastated to lose the investment, it's too big.

Think: tuition for learning, not bet-the-company deployment.

**Typical Week 2 budget range:** $5,000-$25,000 per experiment

### By End of Week 2, You Should Have:

- [ ] Shortlist of 3 promising AI partners
- [ ] Initial conversations scheduled with each
- [ ] Budget allocated that assumes some will fail
- [ ] Clear pain point each partner would address

---

## WEEK 3: Start One Experiment

**Primary task:** Launch a single pilot with explicit learning goals.

Notice: learning goals, not ROI goals.

Traditional pilots ask "Did this work?"

Grow-With pilots ask "What did we learn?"

### Design for Learning

Your pilot should answer questions like:
→ How does this tool perform with our specific data?
→ What does adoption look like with our specific team?
→ Where are the friction points we'd need to address at scale?
→ What would we do differently in the next experiment?

### The 90-Day Framework

**Week 1-2 of pilot:** Setup + initial training
**Week 3-8 of pilot:** Active use + data collection
**Week 9-12 of pilot:** Evaluation + learning capture

**Week 13:** Decide: scale, pivot, or sunset

### What to Measure

Don't just measure ROI. Measure:

- User adoption rate (who actually uses it vs. who says they will)
- Friction points (where does adoption stall?)
- Data quality requirements (what breaks the AI?)
- Change management needs (who needs support to adopt?)

**A pilot that "fails" but teaches you exactly what to look for in your next partner is more valuable than a pilot that "succeeds" but teaches you nothing about scaling.**

### By End of Week 3, You Should Have:

- [ ] One pilot launched with 90-day timeline
- [ ] 3-5 explicit learning questions defined
- [ ] Weekly check-in meetings scheduled
- [ ] Clear criteria for what "success" and "learning" look like

---

## WEEK 4: Build the Loop

**Primary task:** Create infrastructure for learning and pivoting.

The Grow-With Model isn't a one-time decision. It's an operating rhythm.

### The Four Infrastructure Pieces

**1. FEEDBACK MECHANISM**

How does learning from pilots flow to decision-makers?

**Minimum viable:** Shared doc + 30-minute weekly meeting

**Better:** Structured pilot review template with:
- What we expected
- What actually happened
- What we learned
- What we'll do differently next time

**2. PORTFOLIO VIEW**

How will you track multiple experiments at different stages?

**Simple spreadsheet with:**
- Partner name
- Pain point addressed
- Stage (planning / pilot / scale / sunset)
- Budget allocated
- Learning captured so far

**3. PIVOT PROTOCOL**

When does a pilot get more investment? When does it get sunset?

**Decision criteria:**
- Is it solving the pain point we identified?
- Are users actually adopting it?
- What are we learning that applies beyond this specific tool?

**Don't wait for "proof it works." Look for "proof we're learning."**

**4. KNOWLEDGE BASE**

Where does learning get captured for future experiments?

**Minimum viable:** Shared document with sections for:
- Lessons from failures
- Lessons from successes
- Vendor evaluation criteria we've developed
- Teams that need extra change management support

This doesn't need to be sophisticated. What matters is that learning compounds—that experiment #5 benefits from experiments #1-4.

### By End of Week 4, You Should Have:

- [ ] Weekly 30-minute portfolio review meeting scheduled
- [ ] Shared doc for capturing learnings
- [ ] Clear decision criteria for when to scale vs. sunset
- [ ] Portfolio view tracking current and planned experiments

## Common Obstacles + Solutions

### OBSTACLE 1: "We can't get budget for unproven solutions"

**Reframe the conversation.**

You're not asking for budget for unproven solutions. You're asking for learning budget—R&D for understanding how AI works in your specific context.

**Compare to:**
- What competitors are investing
- The cost of arriving 2-4 years late to a compounding learning curve
- The cost of the $680K retailer who spent four years learning nothing

### OBSTACLE 2: "Our IT/security team won't approve unknown vendors"

**Start with sandboxed pilots.**

Use non-sensitive data. Build the track record that earns expanded access.

**Meanwhile, document the shadow AI already happening—which IT can't see and can't govern.**

Framing changes from "let's try risky new vendors" to "let's bring existing usage under governance."

### OBSTACLE 3: "Leadership wants guaranteed ROI before approving"

**Present the portfolio math.**

No single AI experiment has guaranteed ROI.

But a portfolio of experiments has expected value—and that expected value, according to HBR research, is strongly positive for early movers ($3.70 per $1 invested).

**The question isn't whether any specific experiment will succeed.**

**The question is whether learning now is more valuable than learning later.**

The math says yes.

## Realistic Expectations

### What NOT to Expect:
- Immediate ROI from your first pilot
- Zero failures in your AI portfolio
- Buy-in from everyone in your organization
- AI solutions that work perfectly out of the box

### What TO Expect:
- Learning that informs your second pilot
- Some failures that teach you what to avoid
- Growing internal capability and confidence
- Compound advantage over organizations still waiting

**The goal isn't to get AI right on the first try.**

**The goal is to start the learning curve that compounds.**
