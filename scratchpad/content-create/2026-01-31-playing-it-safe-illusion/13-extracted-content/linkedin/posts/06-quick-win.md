Your organization is already using AI.

You just can't see it.

Here's how to find it in one week—and turn invisible risk into visible opportunity.

---

## The Uncomfortable Fact

According to Salesforce's 2024 State of IT report, 71% of employees are using AI tools right now.

With or without corporate strategy.
With or without IT approval.
With or without your knowledge.

This isn't a prediction for your organization. This is almost certainly already true.

**Your "wait and see" approach isn't preventing AI adoption.**

**It's making that adoption invisible and ungoverned.**

---

## Why This Matters

Shadow AI tells you two critical things:

1. **Where your organization has pain points that AI could address** — If employees are using ChatGPT to draft client communications, that's a signal about where your official tools are failing them.

2. **Where your people are already learning** — Learning you could be capturing, scaling, and governing instead of letting it scatter across personal subscriptions and browser tabs.

Every day that learning stays invisible is a day you're not compounding it.

---

## Week 1 Action: The Shadow AI Audit

This isn't an enforcement exercise. It's a learning exercise.

**Time required:** 3-4 hours spread across one week

**What you'll need:** Curiosity and a willingness to ask questions without judgment

---

## The Process

**DAY 1-2: Map the landscape**

Talk to 5 employees across different departments.

Ask three questions:
1. "What AI tools are you using in your work?"
2. "What problems are those tools solving for you?"
3. "What would you lose if those tools disappeared tomorrow?"

Listen without judgment. You're not here to enforce policy. You're here to understand where capability is already emerging.

**DAY 3-4: Identify patterns**

Document what you heard:

→ Which tools appear multiple times? (Signals: valuable + worth evaluating officially)
→ Which problems keep surfacing? (Signals: pain points AI could address at scale)
→ Which teams are most advanced? (Signals: early adopters who could help others)

**DAY 5: Create your starting map**

You should now have:
- 3-5 AI tools employees are already using
- 3-5 specific pain points those tools address
- 2-3 teams or individuals who are ahead of the curve

**This is your roadmap.**

---

## What to Do With What You Find

**IMMEDIATE (This Week):**

Don't shut down the shadow AI. Learn from it.

The tools employees chose reveal what your organization actually needs—not what vendors think you need.

**SHORT-TERM (Next Month):**

Evaluate the most common shadow AI tools officially.

Can you:
→ Bring them under governance?
→ Negotiate enterprise pricing?
→ Capture the learning that's already happening?

**MEDIUM-TERM (Next Quarter):**

Use shadow AI patterns to identify your first official AI partnerships.

If 12 people are using the same AI writing tool, that's a signal about where to invest—not a problem to solve.

---

## The Advantage This Creates

While your competitors are still debating whether to run AI pilots, you're identifying where AI is already generating value.

While they're waiting for vendors to prove themselves, you're learning from the proof your people have already generated.

**This is the Grow-With Model in action:**

You're not starting from zero. You're starting from wherever your people already are—then building from there.

---

## The One Thing Not to Do

Don't turn this into an enforcement exercise.

If employees feel like you're auditing to shut things down, they'll hide the tools. You'll lose the learning.

**Frame it as:**
"We want to understand what's working so we can support it better."

**Not as:**
"We need to know who's using unauthorized tools."

The goal is to surface learning, not suppress it.

---

By the end of Week 1, you'll have:
