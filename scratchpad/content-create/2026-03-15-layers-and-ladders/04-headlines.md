## Set 1 — The Framework Anchor

**Eyebrow:** Why your AI investments keep dying before they deliver
**Headline:** Layers & Ladders: The Framework That Separates AI Winners from the 80% Who Fail
**Deck:** Most organizations skip straight to AI tools. This diagnostic framework names the six structural layers — and the six maturity rungs — that determine whether AI delivers or dies.

**Headline Analysis:**
- Curiosity: Yes — "7-Layer Map" creates an open loop; reader needs to know what the layers are
- Pain Point: Yes — "investments keep dying" and "80% who fail" mirror the VP/Director watching pilots collapse
- Promise: Yes — a diagnostic framework that reveals the specific problem
- Specificity: Yes — "7-Layer," "80%," named mechanism ("diagnostic framework")
- Big 4 Sentiments: New (a map they haven't seen), Safe (diagnostic before investment)
- Strongest Element: The word "Map" signals structure and clarity — exactly what an overwhelmed operator craves. Combined with the 80% failure stat, it positions this content as the compass they've been missing.

## Set 2 — The Penthouse Metaphor

**Deck:** The Layers & Ladders framework reveals the six structural floors AI requires — and the six-rung maturity ladder that shows exactly where you are in the climb.

**Headline Analysis:**
- Curiosity: Yes — "what goes underneath — and in what order" demands the click
- Pain Point: Yes — "watched an AI pilot quietly die" is visceral and specific to ICP experience
- Promise: Yes — reveals the structural order required for AI success
- Specificity: Yes — "seven-story building," "in what order" implies sequenced instructions
- Big 4 Sentiments: New (penthouse metaphor reframes the problem), Safe (building in the right order)
- Strongest Element: The penthouse metaphor does instant diagnostic work. The reader sees their own mistake in a single image — they tried to build the top floor first. No jargon. No abstraction. Just a picture that lands.

## Set 3 — The Attribution Reframe

**Eyebrow:** The real reason 80% of AI projects fail
**Headline:** AI Didn't Fail Your Organization. Your Organization Failed AI.
**Deck:** The Layers & Ladders framework maps the six structural foundations AI requires — and shows which gap is responsible for every stalled pilot, wasted budget, and abandoned initiative.

**Headline Analysis:**
- Curiosity: Yes — the reversal ("your organization failed AI") forces a double-take
- Pain Point: Yes — "stalled pilot, wasted budget, abandoned initiative" names the exact wounds
- Promise: Yes — six identifiable gaps that explain failure (and by implication, can be fixed)
- Specificity: Yes — "80%," "Six structural gaps," three named failure modes
- Big 4 Sentiments: Big (reframes the entire AI failure narrative), New (shifts blame from technology to structure)
- Strongest Element: The chiasmus — flipping "AI failed you" into "you failed AI" — creates instant cognitive friction. It's provocative enough to stop the scroll but accurate enough to survive scrutiny. The deck immediately resolves the tension with a concrete, non-blaming explanation.

## Set 5 — The Process Layer

**Eyebrow:** The AI foundation layer nobody builds
**Headline:** Your Processes Are Invisible. That's Why Your AI Keeps Breaking.
**Deck:** Inside the Layers & Ladders framework, Process Intelligence is the silent killer — and the one layer, once built, that makes every agent, copilot, and automation finally stick.

**Headline Analysis:**
- Curiosity: Yes — "one layer fixes all three" creates a strong open loop
- Pain Point: Yes — "undocumented workflows, undefined handoffs, tribal knowledge" names the daily frustration of operations leaders with surgical precision
- Promise: Yes — a single layer (Process Intelligence) that resolves the root cause
- Specificity: Yes — three named failure modes, "one layer," concrete operational language
- Big 4 Sentiments: New (Process Intelligence as a named layer), Easy (one layer, not a complete overhaul)
- Strongest Element: This set works because it names the invisible problem. Every VP of Operations knows their processes live in people's heads — but nobody has told them this is the reason their AI breaks. The three-item list in the deck reads like a diagnostic checklist. The reader either nods at all three or realizes they can't answer confidently — both responses drive the click.

## Recommended Selection

**Primary recommendation: Set 2 (The Penthouse Metaphor)** — The visual metaphor does the most work in the fewest words. It's instantly shareable, requires zero domain knowledge to understand, and creates a memorable anchor the reader carries through the entire 3,000+ word piece.

**Strong runner-up: Set 3 (The Attribution Reframe)** — The chiasmus headline has the highest scroll-stopping power on LinkedIn and social. If the distribution strategy prioritizes social sharing and debate, this set outperforms Set 2.

**Pair consideration:** Set 2 headline with Set 5 deck copy. The penthouse metaphor grabs attention; the process layer specificity seals the click. Example:

> **Eyebrow:** For leaders who watched an AI pilot quietly die
> **Headline:** Stop Building the Penthouse on a One-Story Foundation
> **Deck:** Undocumented workflows, undefined handoffs, and tribal knowledge are silently killing every automation you launch. Here's the layer nobody builds — and how to fix it.
