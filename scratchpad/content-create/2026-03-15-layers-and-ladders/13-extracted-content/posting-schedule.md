### Own Community (10% of engagement time — 1 hour/week)

**Response Strategy:**

**Golden Rule:** Reply to every substantive comment within 1 hour of posting, with priority given to:
1. Questions (highest priority—these are warm leads)
2. Objections (second priority—engagement momentum)
3. Agreements (third priority—validate)
4. Diagnostic requests (fourth priority—soft sales)

**Response Templates by Comment Type:**

**Template 1 — Comment Type: Question ("How do I score my layers?")**
> Great question. Here's the diagnostic: Score yourself 1-5 on each layer:
> - Infrastructure: Can your systems run, scale, and connect?
> - Data: Is your data governed and accessible?
> - Process: Are workflows defined and measurable?
> - Governance: Do you have guardrails and compliance?
> - People: Is your team ready to collaborate with AI?
> - Strategy: Is AI aligned to business outcomes?
>
> Your lowest score is your ceiling. Reply back with your lowest-scoring layer—let's identify what's blocking the next rung.

**Template 2 — Comment Type: Objection ("This sounds too complex")**
> This is exactly the objection I hear most—because it *is* complex. But here's the paradox: ignoring the complexity costs more than facing it.
>
> The organizations winning at AI didn't try to solve all six layers at once. They:
> 1. Diagnosed the weakest layer (1 week)
> 2. Fixed that single layer (varies, 4-12 weeks typically)
> 3. Then moved up
>
> One layer. One rung. That's the path that actually works. Which layer feels like the biggest blocker for you?

**Template 3 — Comment Type: Specific Example ("We had this with Copilot")**
> This is a *perfect* example of the pattern. Three layers were weak at once:
> - No defined workflows (Layer 3 gap)
> - No training on how to use it (Layer 5 gap)
> - No success metrics tied to use cases (Layer 6 gap)
>
> When one layer is weak, you get adoption resistance. When three are weak together, you get the abandonment you saw.
>
> If you were going to fix *one* of those three for the next pilot, which layer would unlock the most value for your team?

**Template 4 — Comment Type: Validation ("This is exactly our problem")**
> You're recognizing the pattern—that's huge. Most organizations live in that pain without naming it. Naming it is the first step to fixing it.
>
> The diagnostic is free and takes 2 hours. You'll know exactly which layer is your ceiling after that. Want to share what your preliminary lowest-scoring layer is? Happy to help you see what fixing it would unlock.

**Template 5 — Comment Type: Tier 2 Creator Engagement ("Connecting your framework to my work")**
> [Acknowledge specific connection] This is where the Layers framework sits—it's the diagnostic that makes your work actionable. When teams know *which layer* is blocking them, they can prioritize with precision.
>
> Would love to explore how the layers map to [their domain/expertise]. The more specific we get, the more people can self-diagnose and take action.

**Engagement Cadence:**
- Check comments during your workday (morning, lunch, afternoon)
- Prioritize Tier 1 questions and engagement within 1 hour
- Create 1-2 pinned responses if there's a high-volume comment pattern
- Monitor for objections and address publicly (increases visibility)
