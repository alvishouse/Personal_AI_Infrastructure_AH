The old winners deployed first.

The new winners build first.

The race used to reward speed. First to ship, first to win. That logic made sense when the competitive edge was access to technology. It does not hold anymore. The technology is widely available. The edge is structural readiness.

The leaders who win at AI are not the ones with the fastest pilots.

They diagnose before they invest. They document before they automate. They design the handoffs before they delegate.

That sequence changes the math entirely. Every investment compounds instead of collapses. Every layer you build strengthens the one above it. Every handoff you define is one less point of failure.

The organizations still chasing the penthouse — the AI copilots, the autonomous agents, the demo that impressed the board — will keep cycling through $500,000 to $2,000,000 failures until they figure this out.

You do not have to wait.

Build the floors first.
