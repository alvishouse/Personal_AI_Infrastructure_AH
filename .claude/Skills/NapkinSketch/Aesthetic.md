# Napkin Sketch Visual Aesthetic System

**"Ideas captured in the moment, imperfections included."**

A visual system emulating spontaneous analog sketches on napkins, envelopes, or scrap paper - the ultimate low-fidelity medium where great ideas begin.

---

## Core Concept: Spontaneous Ideation

Every visualization captures **raw thinking in real-time** — the energy of a brainstorm session when an idea strikes and you grab whatever's nearby to sketch it out.

**The Philosophy:** *"First draft energy, unfiltered brilliance."*
- Paper texture is part of the story
- Single tool (pen or marker), used confidently
- Mistakes left visible, showing iteration
- Gestural lines reveal momentum
- All-caps handwriting for urgency

---

## The Napkin Sketch Look

### What We Want
- **Paper napkin texture** — Visible grain, creases, folds
- **Ink bleed quality** — Pen ink slightly absorbed into paper fibers
- **Single tool consistency** — Ballpoint pen OR Sharpie, not both
- **Gestural confidence** — Quick, bold strokes without hesitation
- **Visible corrections** — Scribbled-out mistakes, not erased
- **All-caps handwriting** — Hurried but readable
- **Coffee stains optional** — Adds authenticity (if appropriate)

### Reference Styles
- **Back-of-napkin sketches** — The literal inspiration
- **Ballpoint pen on paper** — Quick blue ink sketches
- **Sharpie on envelope** — Bold black marker ideation
- **Restaurant napkin brainstorms** — Coffee shop planning sessions
- **Spontaneous wireframes** — First-draft UI concepts

### What to AVOID
❌ Clean white paper
❌ Perfect circles or straight lines
❌ Digital precision
❌ Multiple mixed media (pick pen OR marker)
❌ Erased or cleaned-up corrections
❌ Typed fonts
❌ Corporate polish

**If it looks planned or refined, it's wrong.**

## Linework & Texture Rules

This is where the authenticity lives.

### Line Quality
- **Quick and confident** — Drawn fast without hesitation
- **Variable pressure** — Thicker where pen pressed hard
- **Gestural energy** — Shows momentum of hand movement
- **Ink bleed** — Slightly absorbed into paper at heavy pressure points
- **No perfect curves** — Circles as spirals or overlapping loops

### Paper Texture (Critical Element)
- **Napkin grain visible** — Texture throughout entire image
- **Creases and folds** — Paper isn't perfectly flat
- **Worn edges** — May have tears or worn corners
- **Fiber texture** — Visible paper weave
- **Absorbent quality** — Ink spreads slightly into fibers

### Correction Style
- **Scribbled out** — Quick scratch marks over mistakes
- **Not erased** — Corrections left visible
- **Arrows redirect** — Show thought process evolving
- **Layered sketches** — Multiple attempts visible
- **Cross-outs** — Single or double strike-through

### Texture
- **Paper grain always visible** — Never smooth digital background
- **Coffee ring stains** — Optional but authentic
- **Grease marks** — If appropriate for napkin context
- **Uneven ink** — Some areas darker where pen dwelled

### Absolute Rule
> **The medium is the message. Paper texture and ink authenticity are non-negotiable.**
> This should look like it was found, not created.

## Typography Rules

### Handwriting Style
**All-Caps Hurried Script** — Quick handwriting with urgency

### Characteristics
- ALL CAPS for speed and readability
- Slightly irregular letter height
- Quick pen strokes, not carefully formed
- Readable but shows haste
- Authentic handwriting imperfection

### Usage
| Context | Style | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Labels | All-caps, medium | Quick identification |
| Notes | All-caps, small | Supporting details |
| Titles | All-caps, larger/underlined | Section headers |
| Emphasis | Circled or boxed text | Call attention |

### Handwriting Rules
- No perfect letter formation
- Baseline slightly wavy
- Some letters rushed or abbreviated
- Pen script only (never typed)
- Underlines are quick strokes

**Authenticity Principle:**
> Real handwriting has personality. Letters aren't uniform.

---

## Composition Rules

### Layout Principles
1. **Central sketch** — Main idea at center or upper third
2. **Annotations surround** — Labels and notes orbit main sketch
3. **Asymmetric placement** — Natural, not grid-aligned
4. **Margins ignored** — Sketches extend to edges if needed

### Balance
- **Organic flow** — Follows thought process
- **Visual weight natural** — Main concept clear but casual
- **Napkin constraints** — Limited real estate, use it all

### Element Limits
| Element Type | Guidelines |
|--------------|------------|
| Main sketches | 1-3 per napkin |
| Arrows | Bold, gestural |
| Text annotations | Keep brief |
| Corrections | 1-3 visible revisions |

### Flow
- **Non-linear okay** — Follows brainstorm process
- **Arrows show relationships** — Bold directional cues
- **Clustering** — Related ideas grouped naturally

## Visual Metaphors

### How to Translate Concepts

| Concept Type | Visual Strategy | Style Notes |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------|
| System Architecture | Quick boxes with connecting lines | Gestural arrows, confident strokes |
| Data Flow | Bold arrows with hurried labels | Show movement with pen pressure |
| Process Steps | Numbered circles or boxes | Sequential but casual |
| Hierarchies | Tree sketched quickly | Imperfect branches |
| Relationships | Network with pen lines | Quick connection strokes |
| Wireframes | Rough UI boxes | Minimal detail, concept only |

### The Formula
```
1. Choose your tool (ballpoint blue OR Sharpie black)
2. Sketch central concept with confident strokes
3. Add gestural arrows and connections
4. Label with all-caps hurried handwriting
5. Leave 1-2 visible corrections (scribbled out)
6. Add paper texture throughout
7. Optional: coffee ring stain for authenticity
8. Ensure napkin/paper grain visible
9. Verify it looks spontaneous, not planned
```
