# Da Vinci Style

## Style Overview
**"Leonardo da Vinci Technical Notebook"**

Technical sketch on aged parchment in Leonardo da Vinci's notebook style. Centuries-old engineering drawings and mechanical studies with visible construction geometry, cross-hatching, and intellectual annotations. Renaissance genius meets timeless technical documentation.

## Core Aesthetic
- **Aged parchment paper** with visible grain
- **Hand-drawn deliberate imperfection** (not digital precision)
- **Cross-hatching for all shading** (NO solid fills)
- **Visible construction geometry** (compass marks, guide lines)
- Leonardo's characteristic technical sketch style

## When to Use This Style
- Content requiring timeless intellectual depth
- Technical concepts with historical gravitas
- Strategic frameworks needing authority
- Mechanical or systems thinking visualization
- Content that bridges past wisdom with future thinking

---

## COLOR PALETTE

### Background
- **Parchment beige:** #ECE6D9 (warm aged paper)
- Natural paper aging with slight yellowing
- Visible grain throughout
- Centuries-old technical drawing quality

### Primary Ink
- **Deep slate blue:** #3B546B
- All primary linework, text, circles, cross-hatching
- Leonardo's characteristic ink color
- Variable line weight (heavier on emphasis, lighter on guides)

### Accent Color
- **Burnt copper:** #CF5828
- Used sparingly (5-10% of composition)
- Highlights key elements:
  - Important mechanical components
  - Motion indicators
  - Critical annotations
  - Emphasis elements

---

## ILLUSTRATION TECHNIQUE

### Line Quality
- Hand-drawn with deliberate imperfection
- Variable line weight (NOT uniform)
- Multiple overlapping strokes showing study process
- Visible construction: compass marks, guide lines, radial symmetry
- Exploratory quality (Leonardo studying the subject)

### Cross-Hatching (CRITICAL)
- ALL shading done with cross-hatching
- NO solid color fills ever
- NO gradients ever
- Parallel lines at angles for value
- Denser hatching = darker areas
- Lighter hatching = mid-tones
- Bare parchment = highlights
- Hatching follows form (curved on cylinders, straight on planes)

### Construction Geometry
- Compass center points visible (small dots or crosses)
- Guide lines showing proportions
- Radial lines for symmetry
- Construction circles showing how circles were drawn
- Geometric foundation visible beneath finished work

### Subject Matter
- Mechanical systems and devices
- Human figures with anatomical study quality
- Strategic frameworks as mechanical systems
- Interconnected loops and flywheel concepts
- Collaboration scenarios (human + machine)

## TYPOGRAPHY

### ⚠️ KNOWN MODEL BEHAVIOR — MUST OVERRIDE
**Models default to generating Italian text** when prompted with "Leonardo da Vinci" style because he was Italian. This is WRONG for PAI. Every prompt using Da Vinci style MUST include the English-only constraint prominently — both at the start and as a negative flag at the end. Use explicit English examples in the prompt (e.g., "labeled ROTATIONAL FORCE not forza rotatoria") to force English output.

### Text Style
- Hand-lettered Leonardo script (italicized, precise)
- **ALL TEXT MUST BE IN ENGLISH** (MANDATORY — models default to Italian, override explicitly)
- NO Latin, Italian, or mirror-script under any circumstances
- When in doubt: spell out the English word you want to see in the image
- Technical annotations with arrow indicators
- Measurements and ratios in margins
- Small margin notes showing intellectual curiosity

### Text Characteristics
- Slate blue ink (#3B546B) primary
- Copper accent (#CF5828) for emphasis labels
- Flowing italic script
- Labels positioned with arrows to subjects
- Intellectual depth visible in annotations

## COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Layout Principles
- Central subject occupies 40-60% of frame
- Supporting studies in margins
- Annotations orbit around main subject
- Generous parchment margins showing
- Asymmetric but balanced

### Common Elements
- Main technical study (center)
- Detail callouts (corners or margins)
- Exploded views showing mechanisms
- Multiple angles or perspectives
- Cross-sectional views
- Annotation clusters

## CRITICAL STYLE CHARACTERISTICS

### Mandatory Elements
- Parchment texture visible throughout
- Cross-hatching for ALL shading (no solid fills)
- Visible construction geometry
- Hand-drawn deliberate imperfection
- Variable line weight
- Leonardo script annotations **IN ENGLISH ONLY**
- Intellectual curiosity visible
- Centuries-old quality
- ALL text must be in English (no Latin, Italian, or mirror-script)

### Forbidden Elements
- Solid color fills
- Gradients
- Digital vector precision
- Perfect circles (show construction)
- Clean modern lines
- Typed fonts
- White paper background
- Photorealism
- Latin or Italian text
- Mirror-script or reversed text
- Any non-English text
