# LinkedIn Hook Structure & Feed Visibility Guide

## Core Principle

**A compelling hook that gets cut off is worthless.**

The most brilliant hook in the world means nothing if your audience never sees it. LinkedIn's feed truncation mechanics mean you have approximately **2 lines visible before "see more"** appears.

**Your job:** Pack maximum compelling power into minimum visible space.

---

## Part 1: LinkedIn Feed Mechanics (The Sanders Methodology)

### The Visibility Window

**LinkedIn Feed Truncation:**
- **Line 1:** ~65 characters visible
- **Line 2:** ~40 characters visible
- **Everything else:** Hidden behind "see more"

**What This Means:**
You have ~105 characters total to:
1. Stop the scroll
2. Create curiosity
3. Compel the click

**If your hook starts at character 106, nobody sees it.**

### The Math

```
VISIBLE ZONE (0-105 chars)
├─ Line 1: Characters 1-65 (CRITICAL)
└─ Line 2: Characters 66-105 (SUPPORTING)

HIDDEN ZONE (106+ chars)
└─ Everything after "see more" click
```

**Strategy:** The visible zone must work as a complete hook on its own.

## Part 2: Adapted Headline Structure for LinkedIn

### From Cornerstone to LinkedIn

Your content creation system uses the **Eyebrow + Headline + Deck Copy** structure for long-form content. Here's how it adapts to LinkedIn's feed constraints:

**Cornerstone Structure (Long-Form):**
```
Eyebrow: "For Busy Professionals…"
Headline: "Amazing Secret Discovered By One-Legged Golfer..."
Deck Copy: "Now you can learn to use your natural ability..."
```

**LinkedIn Adaptation (Feed-Optimized):**
```
Line 1 (0-65 chars): [Eyebrow + Truncated Headline]
Line 2 (66-105 chars): [Hook Continuation]
Line 3+: [Full Development]
```

### The LinkedIn Hook Formula

**LINE 1 (65 chars max):**
- Identity filter OR contrarian statement
- Must create pattern interrupt
- Must be self-contained (works alone if Line 2 is cut)

**LINE 2 (40 chars max):**
- Amplify Line 1's tension
- Add specificity or mechanism
- Create irresistible curiosity gap

**LINE 3+ (Hidden until click):**
- Deliver on the promise
- Develop the full argument
- Use full headline structure (eyebrow/headline/deck principles)

## Part 3: The 7 Headline Elements for LinkedIn

From your cornerstone headline generation system, these elements must appear in the **visible zone (Lines 1-2)**:

### 1. Curiosity
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** The contradiction, unusual mechanism, or unexpected authority
```
✅ GOOD: "Singapore just did something stability-obsessed economies never do."
❌ BAD: "I want to talk about Singapore's new AI policy today."
```

### 2. Call Out Pain Point
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** Name the frustration in the identity filter
```
✅ GOOD: "For leaders watching AI closely but moving cautiously:"
❌ BAD: "This is for people interested in AI."
```

### 3. Promise Solution
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** Implied in the contrarian take
```
✅ GOOD: "The cautious path and the failing path are the same path."
(Implication: There's a different path that works)
```

### 4. Specificity
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** Numbers, names, timeframes in visible zone
```
✅ GOOD: "Singapore committed over $1 billion to unproven AI players."
❌ BAD: "Singapore made a big AI investment recently."
```

### 5. Simplicity
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** One clear idea per visible line
```
✅ GOOD: "Playing it safe with AI reduces our risk of failure."
         "That's the myth."
❌ BAD: "There are several interconnected factors that contribute to..."
```

### 6. Credibility/Address Skepticism
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** Authority anchor in Line 1-2
```
✅ GOOD: "MIT and BCG's 2024 study found..."
✅ GOOD: "After analyzing 500+ AI implementations..."
```

### 7. Time Frame
**LinkedIn Adaptation:** Urgency or timeline in first 105 chars
```
✅ GOOD: "The 2-year head start everyone's looking for doesn't come from waiting."
❌ BAD: (Buried in paragraph 4)
```

## Part 4: The Big 4 Sentiments (LinkedIn Feed Context)

From your Big Idea prompt, these sentiments must be **felt** in the visible zone:

### NEW (Most Critical)
**Visible Zone Strategy:** Contrarian angle or fresh mechanism
```
Example:
"Singapore's most stability-obsessed economy just partnered with unproven AI players." [69 chars]

Sentiment Conveyed: This is unprecedented, only happening now, novel decision
```

### EASY
**Visible Zone Strategy:** Simplicity signal or single action
```
Example:
"The shift from 'playing it safe' to 'learning fast' requires one mental reframe." [82 chars]

Sentiment Conveyed: Not complex, accessible, doable
```

### SAFE
**Visible Zone Strategy:** Authority backing or proven pattern
```
Example:
"MIT's 2024 research: AI strategies show 80% success. No strategy? 37%." [72 chars]

Sentiment Conveyed: Data-backed, proven, predictable
```

### BIG
**Visible Zone Strategy:** Scale, consequence, or importance signal
```
Example:
"In AI economics, waiting for proof is the proof you'll arrive too late." [73 chars]

Sentiment Conveyed: High stakes, significant impact, groundbreaking
```

## Part 5: LinkedIn Hook Templates (Feed-Optimized)

### Template 1: Authority + Contrarian
```
LINE 1 (Identity + Contrarian):
"[AUTHORITY FIGURE] just did [UNEXPECTED ACTION]." [<65 chars]

LINE 2 (Specificity):
"[SPECIFIC DETAIL that defies expectation]" [<40 chars]

LINE 3+ (Development):
"[Context, implication, framework reveal]"

EXAMPLE:
"Singapore just committed $1B to unproven AI players." [54 chars] ✅
"The world's most cautious economy." [35 chars] ✅
```

### Template 2: Myth-Busting
```
LINE 1 (The Myth):
"The myth: '[COMMON BELIEF]'" [<65 chars]

LINE 2 (The Truth Signal):
"The truth: [CONTRARIAN REALITY]" [<40 chars]

LINE 3+ (Mechanism):
"[Why the myth persists, what actually works]"

EXAMPLE:
"The myth: Playing it safe reduces risk of failure." [52 chars] ✅
"The truth: They're the same path." [34 chars] ✅
```

### Template 3: Pattern Interrupt Story
```
LINE 1 (Unexpected Scenario):
"I [DID THING] that [AUTHORITY/NORM] says never to do." [<65 chars]

LINE 2 (Result Signal):
"Here's what happened:" [<40 chars]

LINE 3+ (Story Development):
"[Full narrative using 3S structure: Structure, Struggle, Solution]"

EXAMPLE:
"I told a client to partner with an AI startup with zero customers." [68 chars] ⚠️ (Trim by 3)
"Here's what happened next:" [27 chars] ✅
```

### Template 4: Data-Driven Authority
```
LINE 1 (Credential + Finding):
"After [ANALYZING X], I discovered [KEY INSIGHT]." [<65 chars]

LINE 2 (The Gap):
"Most leaders miss this completely." [<40 chars]

LINE 3+ (Framework Reveal):
"[What they miss, what works instead, your framework]"

EXAMPLE:
"After analyzing 500+ AI implementations, I found a pattern." [60 chars] ✅
"Winners do one thing differently." [33 chars] ✅
```

### Template 5: Question Hook
```
LINE 1 (Provocative Question):
"What if [CONVENTIONAL WISDOM] is backwards?" [<65 chars]

LINE 2 (Tease):
"New research suggests it is." [<40 chars]

LINE 3+ (Evidence & Framework):
"[Research, mechanism, alternative approach]"

EXAMPLE:
"What if 'waiting for proof' guarantees you'll arrive too late?" [63 chars] ✅
"Singapore's $1B bet says yes." [30 chars] ✅
```

## Part 7: Hook Writing Process

### Step 1: Write Full Hook (Ignore Limits)

Start with your cornerstone headline structure. Get the complete thought down:

```
FULL THOUGHT:
"For directors and executives who have been watching AI transformation closely but moving cautiously because they want to see proven results before committing resources: Singapore's government just made a decision that challenges everything you think you know about playing it safe in AI adoption."
```

### Step 2: Extract Core Tension

Identify the single most compelling element:

```
CORE TENSION:
"Singapore (ultra-cautious) committed to unproven AI"
```

### Step 3: Build Visible Zone (105 chars)

**Line 1 (65 chars):** The core tension
```
"Singapore just committed $1B to 'unproven' AI players." [55 chars]
```

**Line 2 (40 chars):** Amplify the contradiction
```
"The world's most cautious economy." [35 chars]
```

**Total Visible:** 90 characters ✅

### Step 4: Develop Hidden Zone

Use full headline structure (eyebrow/headline/deck) for Lines 3+:

```
LINE 3: "Read that again. *Unproven players.*"

LINE 4-6: [Eyebrow equivalent - Identity filter]
"Singapore doesn't gamble. For decades, their strategy was simple: wait for technologies to mature elsewhere, then recruit proven winners. It worked brilliantly."

LINE 7-9: [Headline equivalent - The shift]
"Until AI changed the rules."

LINE 10+: [Deck equivalent - What they discovered]
"Here's what they discovered that most organizations still haven't:"
[Continue with framework...]
```

### Step 5: Test Character Counts

**Tool:**
```bash
echo "Your Line 1 text here" | wc -c
echo "Your Line 2 text here" | wc -c
```

**Manual Check:**
- Line 1: Count characters (spaces count)
- Target: 50-65 characters
- Line 2: Count characters
- Target: 30-40 characters

## Part 8: Quality Checklist

### Before Publishing

**VISIBLE ZONE CHECK (Lines 1-2):**
- [ ] Line 1 ≤ 65 characters
- [ ] Line 2 ≤ 40 characters
- [ ] Lines 1-2 create complete hook (work alone if needed)
- [ ] Pattern interrupt present in visible zone
- [ ] At least ONE of: curiosity, specificity, authority
- [ ] Reads naturally (not choppy due to character limits)

**HEADLINE ELEMENTS CHECK:**
- [ ] Curiosity (visible)
- [ ] Pain point or identity filter (visible or Line 3)
- [ ] Promise solution (implied in visible zone)
- [ ] Specificity (number, name, or timeframe in Lines 1-2)
- [ ] Simplicity (one clear idea per line)
- [ ] Credibility (authority signal present)
- [ ] Time frame (if relevant)

**BIG 4 SENTIMENTS CHECK:**
- [ ] NEW: Feels novel, fresh, or contrarian
- [ ] EASY: Accessible and understandable
- [ ] SAFE: Backed by authority or data
- [ ] BIG: Significant, important, high-stakes

**ENGAGEMENT OPTIMIZATION:**
- [ ] First line stops mid-scroll
- [ ] Second line creates "need to know more"
- [ ] Hidden content delivers on promise
- [ ] CTA clear at end of post
- [ ] Question or engagement prompt included

## Part 9: Common Mistakes & Fixes

### Mistake 1: Burying the Lead

**WRONG:**
```
LINE 1: "I've been thinking a lot about AI adoption strategies lately." [63 chars] ✅ Length OK
LINE 2: "Here's what I discovered." [26 chars] ✅ Length OK
```

**Problem:** Boring visible zone, no hook until Line 3+

**FIX:**
```
LINE 1: "Singapore committed $1B to AI companies with zero proof." [58 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Here's why that's genius, not reckless." [41 chars] ⚠️ (Trim by 1)
```

### Mistake 2: Explanation Before Hook

**WRONG:**
```
LINE 1: "In today's fast-paced business environment, organizations face..." [67 chars] ❌
```

**Problem:** Corporate speak, no pattern interrupt

**FIX:**
```
LINE 1: "Playing it safe is the biggest risk you're taking." [53 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Here's the data that proves it." [32 chars] ✅
```

### Mistake 3: Vague Contrarian

**WRONG:**
```
LINE 1: "Everything you know about AI is wrong." [39 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Let me explain." [15 chars] ✅
```

**Problem:** Too vague, no specificity

**FIX:**
```
LINE 1: "The 'wait for proof' strategy guarantees you'll be too late." [62 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "MIT's 2024 data shows why." [27 chars] ✅
```

### Mistake 4: Line Breaks in Wrong Place

**WRONG:**
```
LINE 1: "Singapore just did something that should make" [47 chars]
LINE 2: "cautious leaders very uncomfortable." [37 chars]
```

**Problem:** Line break mid-thought, loses impact

**FIX:**
```
LINE 1: "Singapore did something that should terrify cautious leaders." [62 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "They committed $1B to unproven AI." [35 chars] ✅
```

### Mistake 5: Sacrificing Clarity for Character Count

**WRONG:**
```
LINE 1: "SG just bet $1B on unproven AI. Bold? Risky? Neither." [55 chars] ✅
```

**Problem:** Too compressed, loses natural voice

**FIX:**
```
LINE 1: "Singapore committed $1B to unproven AI companies." [51 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Not reckless. Strategic." [25 chars] ✅
```

## Part 10: Advanced Techniques

### Technique 1: The Two-Punch Hook

Use Line 1 for setup, Line 2 for punchline:

```
LINE 1: "I told a CFO to budget for AI failures." [41 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "She doubled our AI investment." [31 chars] ✅
```

**Why It Works:** Creates curiosity gap, unexpected result

### Technique 2: The Data Anchor

Lead with hard number or research:

```
LINE 1: "80% success with AI strategy. 37% without." [44 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "MIT's 2024 study. One number changed everything." [50 chars] ⚠️ (Trim by 10)

OPTIMIZED:
LINE 2: "MIT's 2024 study explains why." [31 chars] ✅
```

### Technique 3: The Identity Mirror

Reflect the reader's exact situation:

```
LINE 1: "For leaders waiting for AI to mature before acting:" [53 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Your wait just cost you 2 years." [33 chars] ✅
```

**Why It Works:** "That's me!" reaction + immediate consequence

### Technique 4: The Authority Borrowing

Use famous example or institution:

```
LINE 1: "Singapore (world's most cautious economy) went all-in." [56 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "On unproven AI. Here's their math." [35 chars] ✅
```

**Why It Works:** "If THEY did it..." reduces perceived risk

### Technique 5: The Question-Statement Combo

Question in Line 1, provocative answer in Line 2:

```
LINE 1: "What if playing it safe is the riskiest move?" [47 chars] ✅
LINE 2: "Singapore's $1B bet says it is." [32 chars] ✅
```

**Why It Works:** Engages reader's brain, provides instant payoff

## Part 11: LinkedIn-Specific Considerations

### Mobile vs Desktop Visibility

**Mobile (80% of LinkedIn users):**
- Line 1: ~55 characters visible (shorter!)
- Line 2: ~35 characters visible
- Strategy: Be even more concise

**Desktop:**
- Line 1: ~65 characters
- Line 2: ~45 characters
- Strategy: Can use full limits

**RECOMMENDATION:** Optimize for mobile (55/35 split)

### Emoji Usage in Hooks

**IN VISIBLE ZONE:**
- Avoid emojis in Lines 1-2
- Reason: Takes up character count, reduces professionalism
- Exception: If brand voice requires (test first)

**IN HIDDEN ZONE (Line 3+):**
- Use sparingly for visual breaks
- Bullets (→, •) acceptable
- Checkmarks (✓) for lists

### Hashtag Placement

**WRONG (Visible Zone):**
```
LINE 1: "#AI #Leadership Singapore just..." [38 chars but CLUTTERED]
```

**RIGHT (End of Post):**
```
[Full post content]
---
#AIStrategy #Leadership #Innovation
```

### Link Preview Impact

If including a link that generates a preview card:
- Preview card appears BELOW your text
- Your full hook (Lines 1-2) remains visible
- Don't sacrifice hook quality for link placement

## Part 12: Testing & Optimization

### A/B Test Framework

**Test Variables:**
1. Hook structure (contrarian vs question vs data)
2. Character count (55/35 vs 65/40)
3. Specificity level (exact numbers vs rounded)
4. Authority anchor (study vs example vs credential)

**Measure:**
- Click-through rate ("see more" expansion)
- Engagement in first hour
- Golden Ratio: (Comments + Shares) / Likes

**Frequency:** Test one variable per post

### Performance Indicators

**GOOD HOOK METRICS:**
- 15%+ click-through to "see more"
- Comments within first 15 minutes
- Shares > 3% of total engagement
- Golden Ratio > 0.15

**WEAK HOOK METRICS:**
- High likes, low comments (vanity engagement)
- Slow engagement velocity (nothing in first hour)
- Golden Ratio < 0.10

**Optimization Actions:**
- Review top 3 posts: What hook structure worked?
- Review bottom 3 posts: Where did hook fail?
- Document patterns in hook performance log

## Part 13: Hook Writing Workflow

### Daily Extraction Process

**Step 1: Extract from Cornerstone (5 min)**
- Identify section to extract
- Pull complete thought
- Note key elements (data, story, framework step)

**Step 2: Draft Full Hook (5 min)**
- Write without character limits
- Include all headline elements
- Use Big 4 sentiments

**Step 3: Compress to Visible Zone (10 min)**
- Identify single most compelling element
- Build Line 1 (target: 55 chars for mobile)
- Build Line 2 (target: 35 chars for mobile)
- Test: Do Lines 1-2 work alone?

**Step 4: Develop Hidden Content (10 min)**
- Expand on visible hook promise
- Use full headline structure
- Include framework, examples, data
- Add engagement prompt at end

**Step 5: Quality Check (5 min)**
- Run checklist (Part 8)
- Character count verification
- Read aloud for flow
- Check all 7 headline elements present

**Total Time:** 35 minutes per post

## Part 14: Emergency Hook Rescue

If your hook isn't working (low engagement), fix it fast:

### Same-Day Hook Fix

**Within 2 Hours of Posting:**

1. **Check character counts**
   - If exceeding limits, you likely have invisible hook
   - Most impactful fix available

2. **Identify what got cut**
   - What's in your Line 3+ that should be in Lines 1-2?
   - Move the compelling part UP

3. **Rewrite if possible**
   - LinkedIn allows edits
   - Fix character counts
   - Keep existing comments/engagement

**Example Rescue:**

**BEFORE (Low engagement):**
```
"I've been thinking about AI adoption strategies. There's a pattern that keeps appearing in my research that I find fascinating. Let me share what I've discovered." [163 chars - most buried]
```

**AFTER (Rescue):**
```
"80% of AI initiatives succeed with strategy. 37% without." [58 chars] ✅
"MIT's 2024 study. The pattern is clear." [40 chars] ✅

I've been analyzing this research for months. Here's what separates winners from losers...
```

**Result:** Hook now visible, engagement salvageable
