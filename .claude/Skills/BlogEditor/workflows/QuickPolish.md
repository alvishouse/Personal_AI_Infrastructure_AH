# Quick Polish Workflow

**Fast final polish for blog posts that are 90% ready.**

Use this workflow when content has been edited already and just needs final cleanup before publish.

---

## Purpose

Rapid editorial pass focusing on:
- Grammar, spelling, punctuation
- Sentence-level clarity
- Formatting consistency
- Quick scannability check
- Final typo sweep

**Time investment:** Fast, focused. Assumes Big Idea and structure already solid.

---

## When to Use

✅ **Use Quick Polish when:**
- Content has already been through full editorial review
- Structure and thesis are strong
- Just needs final cleanup before publish
- User says "final polish" or "proofread"
- Timeline is tight

❌ **Don't use Quick Polish when:**
- First time reviewing this content (use FullEdit instead)
- Big Idea or structure needs work
- Significant clarity issues exist
- Content requires major revisions

---

## 🚨 QUICK POLISH STEPS

```
INPUT: Near-final blog draft
     ↓
[1] READ: Quick full pass
     ↓
[2] CHECK: Editorial checklist
     ↓
[3] FIX: Apply corrections
     ↓
[4] VERIFY: Final scan
     ↓
OUTPUT: Publish-ready content
```

---

## Step 1: Quick Read

Read through entire draft once, noting:
- Any grammar/spelling errors
- Awkward sentences
- Formatting inconsistencies
- Missing visual breaks

**Don't analyze deeply** - just note what catches your eye.

---

## Step 2: Apply Quick Editorial Checklist

From `EditingFrameworks.md`:

### STRUCTURE (Quick scan):
- [ ] Headline strong and accurate?
- [ ] Opening hook compelling?
- [ ] Subheadings descriptive and scannable?
- [ ] Conclusion with clear takeaway?

### CLARITY (Sentence-level):
- [ ] All sentences clear on first read?
- [ ] No jargon without explanation?
- [ ] Active voice preferred?
- [ ] Average sentence length < 20 words?
- [ ] Paragraphs short (2-4 sentences)?

### STYLE (Grammar & polish):
- [ ] Grammar correct?
- [ ] Spelling correct?
- [ ] Punctuation correct?
- [ ] Consistent tense and POV?
- [ ] No typos or copy-paste errors?

### SCANNABILITY:
- [ ] Subheadings every 3-4 paragraphs?
- [ ] Lists used where appropriate?
- [ ] White space for visual breaks?
- [ ] Bold/italic used sparingly for emphasis?

### FORMATTING:
- [ ] Consistent heading levels (H1 → H2 → H3)?
- [ ] Links formatted correctly?
- [ ] Lists formatted consistently?
- [ ] No stray formatting artifacts?

---

## Step 3: Apply Corrections

**Make direct edits to the draft:**

### Grammar & Spelling:
- Fix all typos
- Correct punctuation
- Ensure subject-verb agreement
- Fix any tense inconsistencies

### Clarity Improvements:
```
Before: "The utilization of AI systems can enable the facilitation of..."
After: "AI systems help..."

Before: "It is important to note that there are several factors which..."
After: "Several factors affect..."
```

### Formatting Fixes:
- Ensure consistent heading hierarchy
- Fix any broken links
- Standardize list formatting
- Remove extra spaces or line breaks

---

## Step 4: Final Verification Scan

**Read through once more quickly:**
- First paragraph: Strong hook?
- Random middle paragraph: Clear and scannable?
- Last paragraph: Clear takeaway?
- Skim subheadings: Tell the story?
- Spot check: Any remaining typos?

## Example Corrections

### Grammar/Style:

```
❌ "There are many companies that are implementing AI"
✅ "Many companies are implementing AI"

❌ "The reason is because..."
✅ "The reason is..." or "This is because..."

❌ "Very unique"
✅ "Unique"

❌ Passive: "AI was implemented by the team"
✅ Active: "The team implemented AI"
```

### Clarity:

```
❌ Long: "In order to achieve the goal of improving performance"
✅ Short: "To improve performance"

❌ Vague: "Things got better"
✅ Specific: "Response time dropped 40%"

❌ Jargon: "Leveraging synergies"
✅ Plain: "Working together"
```

### Scannability:

```
❌ Long paragraph (8 sentences, no breaks)
✅ Split into 2-3 shorter paragraphs with clear topic sentences

❌ Wall of text
✅ Add subheading before dense section

❌ Comma-separated list in prose
✅ Convert to bulleted list
```

---

## Speed Tips

**To make this fast:**

1. **Don't overthink** - Trust your editorial instincts
2. **Focus on obvious issues** - Don't hunt for problems
3. **Use Find & Replace** for repeated issues:
   ```
   - "in order to" → "to"
   - Double spaces → Single space
   - "very" → delete
   ```
4. **Skim, don't study** - You're polishing, not analyzing
5. **Time-box it** - Set 10-15 minute limit
